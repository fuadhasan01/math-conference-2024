Keynote Speakers

Dr. Jane Smith

Expert in Applied Mathematics

Dr. Jane Smith specializes in climate modeling and has over 15 years of experience in the field. She has published numerous papers on the application of mathematical techniques to environmental science and has been a keynote speaker at various international conferences.

Talk Title: "Mathematical Models for Climate Change"

Dr. Smith will discuss innovative mathematical models that predict climate patterns and their implications for global policy.

Background:

  • Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics, Stanford University
  • Lead researcher at the Global Climate Institute
  • Recipient of the 2023 Environmental Science Award

Dr. Alan Brown

Pioneer in Algebraic Theory

Dr. Alan Brown is a recognized expert in algebraic theory, focusing on modern cryptography and its applications in secure communications. He has written several influential books and is a sought-after lecturer in mathematics departments worldwide.

Talk Title: "The Role of Algebra in Modern Cryptography"

Dr. Brown will explore how algebraic structures underlie the cryptographic systems that protect digital communications today.

Background:

  • Ph.D. in Mathematics, Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)
  • Professor at the University of Cambridge
  • Board member of the International Cryptography Society
